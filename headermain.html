Impact of COVID-19 on Microenterprises in India

Introduction

This dashboard presents a snapshot of the status of micro-enterprises in India during the COVID-19 crisis and provides insights into their financial and operational health. It has been developed by the Global Alliance for Mass Entrepreneurship (GAME) and LEAD at Krea University (LEAD), with support from the Centre for Digital Financial Inclusion (CDFI) as part of a larger research initiative that seeks to understand the extent to which COVID-19 has impacted microbusinesses and the coping strategies that they have adopted. The dashboard features visualisations from a panel dataset of over 2000 microbusinesses. Launched in May 2020, the study captures key trends on the impact of the COVID crisis and government-mandated lockdowns on business livelihoods, employment, and the income of small businesses, and their business outlook. Additionally, it captures the economic resilience and market adjustments made by these small businesses to overcome the crisis.

Key collaborators

This study is being conducted in collaboration with organisations across a wide range of stakeholders such as financial institutions, development sector organisations, fintech start-ups, among others. Given the limitations in collecting data in the current context, the sample has been drawn from lists shared by partner organisations that are working with microbusinesses in the field.
